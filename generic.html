I am a junior at Duke University's Trinity College of Arts and Sciences, pursuing majors in Computer Science + Linguistics and Classical Languages, with a minor in Classical Archaeology. With a strong passion for ancient history and archaeology, I have actively engaged in various research and fieldwork projects.

As an undergraduate research assistant and field archaeologist for the Vulci 3000 project, I have implemented novel digital archaeology techniques to study a Roman-Etruscan urban center, examining ancient urbanization. I have also contributed to the Neurocities and Ruinscapes project, reconstructing ancient cities in VR. Additionally, I have utilized 3D modeling, inscription translation, and VR to create an engaging experience of the Vicchio Stele, enhancing our understanding of its context.

In my role as a consultant and teaching assistant for Kyros.ai, I have designed comprehensive sprint programs for high school students, helping them achieve their academic goals. I have also developed project-based curricula and provided private consulting, resulting in significant improvements in students' grades.

As an executive board member and business manager for The Pitchforks of Duke University, I have managed numerous performance opportunities worldwide and implemented successful fundraising strategies, increasing group revenue by 20%. My experience in project management and financial planning has been instrumental in achieving organizational goals.

Furthermore, I have gained valuable software engineering experience as a software engineer intern in Duke University's Code+ Program. Collaborating with the Office of Information Technology, I have developed a student portal integrated with a Package Training system to eliminate student wait times for package pickup. This project involved using technologies such as ReactJS, Docker, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Ruby, and Ruby on Rails.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I have pursued personal projects that showcase my dedication to continuous learning and skill improvement. For instance, I have developed an interactive chatbot called ChatMedea, integrating a parsed dataset of Euripides' Medea with the Diablo GPT model. I have also developed a Java program called Route, which calculates the shortest path between two cities in a graph using Dijkstra's algorithm.

With my strong educational background, diverse range of experiences in research, teaching, and software engineering, I am well-prepared to make a valuable impact as an intern. I am eager to contribute my skills, knowledge, and passion to a company or organization where I can continue to learn and grow while making a positive difference.
